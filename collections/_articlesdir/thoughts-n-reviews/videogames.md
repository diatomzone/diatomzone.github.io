title: "videogames [muffled screaming]"
---

when i think about the garbage bags on the side of the road in yakuza 0, surrounded by bikes and musty boxes and litter tread into the asphalt, i lose my mind because someone MADE that.

entire teams of people spent who knows how long crafting and placing every prop on the map, on capturing the glitz and the grime, and when the sun washes out the night’s golden haze and the naked streets feel dingier than they did in the dark, it’s magic.

so much love went into things as mundane as literal bags of trash, so that you could see them on your screen.

through this reverent lens, i’m struck by how everything i take for granted is, in actuality, so profoundly beautiful.

the ripple and murmur of wind through leaves.

a physics simulation of the pasta you boil for dinner could make a computer explode.

every stranger you’ve never spoken to is a life as intricate as your own, shaped by a network of decisions and coincidences too infinitely complex for any amount of code to capture…

by loving games and the work that goes into them, i’m reminded how, despite everything, simply living and breathing in this world is a miraculous thing.
